Hachiko – The Loyal Dog

JAPAN (1920s-1930s) HACHIKO, AN AKITA DOG IN JAPAN, IS ONE OF THE MOST FAMOUS SYMBOLS OF LOYALTY. HIS OWNER, PROFESSOR HIDESABURO UENO, USED TO TAKE THE TRAIN TO WORK, AND EVERY EVENING, HACHIKO WAITED AT THE SHIBUYA STATION FOR HIS RETURN. ONE DAY, THE PROFESSOR PASSED AWAY AT WORK AND NEVER RETURNED. BUT HACHIKO CONTINUED TO WAIT FOR HIM EVERY SINGLE DAY FOR NINE YEARS AT THE STATION. PEOPLE IN TOKYO WERE SO TOUCHED BY HIS DEVOTION THAT THEY BUILT A STATUE OF HACHIKO OUTSIDE SHIBUYA STATION. HIS STORY WAS LATER TURNED INTO A MOVIE CALLED HACHI: A DOG’S TALE.

DUKE – THE SHELTER DOG

UNITED STATES (2012) DUKE WAS A RESCUE DOG ADOPTED BY A LOVING FAMILY. ONE NIGHT, HE SUDDENLY JUMPED ONTO HIS OWNERS’ BED, SHAKING AND BARKING UNCONTROLLABLY. IT WAS UNUSUAL BEHAVIOR, SO THE FAMILY GOT UP TO CHECK ON THEIR 9-WEEK-OLD BABY, HARPER. TO THEIR SHOCK, THE BABY WAS NOT BREATHING! THEY QUICKLY CALLED AN AMBULANCE, AND PARAMEDICS SAVED THE BABY’S LIFE JUST IN TIME. IF IT WEREN’T FOR DUKE’S URGENT WARNING, THE CHILD MIGHT NOT HAVE SURVIVED. DUKE PROVED THAT RESCUE DOGS CAN BECOME LIFESAVERS TOO.

KABANG – THE HEROIC DOG

PHILIPPINES (2012) KABANG, A STREET DOG IN THE PHILIPPINES, BECAME A NATIONAL HERO WHEN SHE SAVED TWO YOUNG GIRLS FROM AN ONCOMING MOTORCYCLE. SHE JUMPED IN FRONT OF THE BIKE, LOSING HER SNOUT IN THE PROCESS, BUT SHE SURVIVED. HER STORY SPREAD WORLDWIDE, AND PEOPLE FROM DIFFERENT COUNTRIES DONATED MONEY FOR HER LIFE-SAVING SURGERY IN THE UNITED STATES. DESPITE HER INJURIES, KABANG LIVED HAPPILY AND CONTINUED TO RECEIVE LOVE FROM PEOPLE ALL OVER THE WORLD.

BRETAGNE – THE 9/11 RESCUE DOG

UNITED STATES (2001-2016) BRETAGNE, A GOLDEN RETRIEVER, WAS ONE OF THE SEARCH-AND-RESCUE DOGS WHO WORKED AT GROUND ZERO AFTER.... THE 9/11 ATTACKS IN NEW YORK. SHE SEARCHED THROUGH THE RUBBLE FOR SURVIVORS AND PROVIDED COMFORT TO FIREFIGHTERS AND FAMILIES AFFECTED BY THE TRAGEDY. EVEN AFTER HER RETIREMENT, BRETAGNE CONTINUED WORKING AS A THERAPY DOG, HELPING CHILDREN LEARN TO READ AND PROVIDING EMOTIONAL SUPPORT TO VETERANS. SHE LIVED UNTIL THE AGE OF 16 AND WAS GIVEN A HERO’S FAREWELL BEFORE PASSING AWAY IN 2016
